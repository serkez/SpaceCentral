A (not so) BRIEF HISTORY OF SPACE EXPLORATION



THE TELESCOPE

Humans have been observing the night sky for as long as we existed. In 1608 a Dutch eyeglass maker became the first person to apply for a patent for a telescope that magnified objects three times. About a year later, Galileo Galilei after hearing about the telescope, created his own that could magnify objects 20 times. He was the first to use the telescope to observe the sky, and he discovered Saturn's rings and four of Jupiter's moons. In 1633 Galileo was sentanced to house arrest after claiming that Earth moved around the Sun. In 1668 Isaac Newton built a reflecting telescope which used mirrors instead of lenses. The development of the radio allowed scientist to study not only light from space, but also other electromagnetic radiation.

ENTER SPACE

During the mid 20th century, there was a "Space Race" between the Soviet Union and the United States. In October of 1957, The Soviet's satellite Sputnik 1 became the first human-made object to orbit Earth. In July of 1969, American Apollo 11 became the first space craft to bring humans to the surface of the moon. Neil Armstong, the commander of Apollo 11 was the first human to step on the lunar surface. During this time period, the Soviet space program achieved the first human spaceflight, the first spacewalk, the first automatic landing, and the first space station.

FAR FROM HOME

The Apollo 13 flight went to the far side of the Moon in 1970. The people aboard hold the record for having traveled the farthest away from Earth than any other humans. Voyager 1 became the first human-made object to leave our Solar System into interstellar space on August 25, 2012. Voyager 1 is currently 11.7 billion miles from Earth, and is the most distant human-made object. The most distant known object from Earth is 13.4 billion light-years away.

THE FUTURE OF SPACE EXPLORATION

The Mars 2020 Perseverance rover and Ingenuity helicopter launched on July 30, 2020, at 7:50 a.m. EDT, and are set to arrive on Mars on February 18, 2021. Persaverance will seek signs of ancient life on Mars as well as collect rock and soil samples to send back to Earth. Ingenuity will mark the first powered flight on another planet. NASA plans on sending astronauts the Moon by 2024, and establishing substainable lunar exploration by 2028. Once we settle on the Moon, we'll be one step closer to stepping foot on Mars. Europa Clipper is set for launch in 2024 and will investigate Jupiter's moon Europa for signs that the moon can support life.
